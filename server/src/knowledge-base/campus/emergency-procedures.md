# Emergency Procedures on Campus

## Who to Call in a Medical Emergency

### Immediate Life-Threatening Emergencies
- **National Emergency Number**: 112 (ambulance, police, fire)
- **Campus Security Control Room**: Available 24/7 — they can dispatch help and contact the on-call doctor
- **Nearest Government Hospital**: The campus security office has the current contact number and can arrange transport

### On-Campus Medical Emergency Steps
1. **Assess safety** — do not put yourself at risk
2. **Call for help** — shout or send someone to get help while you stay with the patient
3. **Call campus security** — they will alert the on-call medical personnel
4. **Do not move** the patient if you suspect a spine, neck, or head injury
5. **Basic first aid** if trained: check airway, breathing, circulation; apply pressure to bleeding wounds
6. **Stay on the line** with the emergency contact until help arrives

## Common Campus Emergency Scenarios

### Unconscious Student
- Check for breathing and pulse
- Place in the recovery position (on their side) if breathing
- Call campus security immediately
- Do not leave the student alone

### Seizure
- Do not restrain the student
- Clear the area of hard objects; place something soft under the head
- Time the seizure — if it lasts more than 5 minutes, call emergency services immediately
- Turn the student on their side after the seizure stops
- Stay with them during the post-seizure confusion period

### Suspected Fracture or Spinal Injury
- Do NOT move the student unless in immediate danger
- Immobilize the area if trained to do so
- Call for emergency medical help immediately

### Severe Allergic Reaction (Anaphylaxis)
- If the student has an epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen): use it immediately in the outer thigh
- Call emergency services immediately even after using the EpiPen
- Lay the student flat with legs elevated (unless breathing is difficult — then sit upright)
- A second EpiPen dose can be given after 5-10 minutes if no improvement

### Student in Mental Health Crisis
- Stay calm and speak in a low, non-threatening voice
- Do not leave the student alone if they express suicidal thoughts
- Call campus counseling center emergency line or campus security
- Do not try to physically restrain unless there is immediate danger to themselves or others

## Sick Leave & Medical Certificates
- The dispensary issues medical certificates for documented illness
- Certificates are accepted by the academic office for attendance condonation
- Request the certificate at the time of your consultation or within 24 hours
