# Ear Pain & Infections

## Symptoms
- Pain inside the ear (otalgia), may be sharp, dull, or throbbing
- Feeling of fullness or pressure in the ear
- Reduced hearing or muffled sounds on the affected side
- Fluid drainage from the ear (clear, yellow, or bloody)
- Itching inside the ear canal (common with swimmer's ear / otitis externa)
- Ringing or buzzing sound in the ear (tinnitus)
- Pain that worsens when pulling on the outer ear or pressing the tragus (ear canal infection)
- Pain that worsens when lying down on the affected side
- Jaw pain or pain when chewing (may be referred pain from TMJ)

## Red Flags
- High fever above 102°F with ear pain (possible serious infection)
- Sudden hearing loss in one or both ears
- Severe vertigo (room spinning) with ear symptoms (possible inner ear involvement)
- Bloody or purulent discharge from the ear
- Swelling, redness, or tenderness of the bone behind the ear (mastoiditis — emergency)
- Facial weakness or drooping on the same side as ear pain (possible facial nerve involvement)
- Ear pain following head trauma, diving, or air travel with sudden onset

## When to Escalate
- If there is swelling or redness behind the ear (mastoiditis risk)
- If there is sudden hearing loss — this needs urgent evaluation
- If severe vertigo accompanies the ear pain
- If ear discharge is bloody or continues for more than 3 days
- If ear pain is severe and not relieved by painkillers
- If the student has a foreign object in the ear — do not attempt removal, refer to doctor

## Self-Care
- Take paracetamol or ibuprofen for pain relief
- Apply a warm compress to the affected ear for 15-20 minutes
- Keep the ear dry — avoid swimming and use a shower cap or cotton ball with petroleum jelly during bathing
- For swimmer's ear prevention: dry ears thoroughly after swimming, use alcohol-based ear drops
- Do not insert cotton swabs, fingers, or any objects into the ear canal
- Sleep with the unaffected ear on the pillow to reduce pressure on the painful ear
- If congestion is contributing: try a decongestant or saline nasal spray
- Chewing gum or swallowing can help equalize pressure (useful for ear pain after flights)
- Avoid loud noise exposure if the ear is sensitive
