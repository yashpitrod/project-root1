# Skin Rashes & Dermatitis

## Symptoms
- Red, inflamed patches of skin that may be raised or flat
- Itching ranging from mild to intense
- Dry, flaky, or scaly skin especially on elbows, knees, or hands
- Small bumps or blisters that may ooze fluid or crust over
- Rash in a specific pattern: ring-shaped (possible fungal), linear (contact dermatitis), or widespread
- Skin irritation worsening after using certain soaps, detergents, or cosmetics
- Rash appearing after wearing new clothing or jewelry
- Warm or painful skin in the affected area

## Red Flags
- Rapidly spreading rash with fever (possible cellulitis or systemic infection)
- Rash that does not blanch when pressed (petechial or purpuric rash — possible meningococcemia)
- Blistering rash around the eyes or near mucous membranes
- Rash with joint pain and fever (possible autoimmune condition)
- Signs of skin infection: increasing redness, warmth, swelling, pus, or red streaks extending from the rash
- Rash following a new medication (possible drug reaction — stop medication and seek evaluation)
- Stevens-Johnson syndrome signs: painful rash with blistering, mucosal involvement, and fever after starting a new medication

## When to Escalate
- If rash is petechial (non-blanching) or purpuric — urgent evaluation needed
- If there are signs of secondary bacterial infection (pus, expanding redness, fever)
- If rash appeared after starting a new medication
- If the student has widespread blistering or involvement of eyes, mouth, or genitals
- If rash has persisted beyond 2 weeks without improvement despite basic care

## Self-Care
- Keep the affected area clean and dry
- Apply over-the-counter hydrocortisone cream (1%) for itching and inflammation (not on face)
- Use fragrance-free, hypoallergenic moisturizers to prevent dryness
- Avoid scratching — trim nails short and consider wearing cotton gloves at night if itching is severe
- For suspected fungal infection (ring-shaped rash, athlete's foot): apply over-the-counter antifungal cream (clotrimazole)
- Wear loose-fitting, breathable cotton clothing
- Switch to mild, fragrance-free soap and detergent
- For contact dermatitis: identify and remove the triggering substance
- Apply cool, damp cloths to the rash for itch relief
