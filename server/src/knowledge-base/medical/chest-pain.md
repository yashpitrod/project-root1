# Chest Pain & Palpitations

## Symptoms
- Tight, squeezing, or pressure-like pain in the chest
- Sharp, stabbing chest pain that worsens with deep breathing or movement
- Rapid or irregular heartbeat (palpitations) — fluttering, pounding, or skipping sensation
- Chest discomfort that comes and goes with exertion or stress
- Mild shortness of breath with chest discomfort
- Muscle soreness in the chest wall after physical activity or coughing
- Burning sensation behind the breastbone (heartburn / acid reflux)
- Anxiety-related chest tightness that resolves with relaxation

## Red Flags
- Crushing or severe chest pain radiating to the left arm, jaw, neck, or back (possible heart attack — emergency)
- Chest pain with sudden shortness of breath, coughing up blood, or leg swelling (possible pulmonary embolism)
- Rapid heart rate above 150 bpm at rest with dizziness or fainting
- Chest pain with loss of consciousness or near-fainting
- Chest pain that does not change with movement, breathing, or position (cardiac pain pattern)
- Severe chest pain in a student with a known heart condition
- Palpitations with shortness of breath, chest pain, and syncope (fainting)
- Any chest pain in a student with a history of cocaine or stimulant use

## When to Escalate
- Any chest pain that cannot be clearly attributed to musculoskeletal cause or acid reflux — refer for evaluation
- If pain radiates to the arm, jaw, or back
- If palpitations are associated with dizziness, fainting, or chest pain
- If the student has a known cardiac condition and develops new symptoms
- If chest pain persists more than 15 minutes without an obvious cause

## Self-Care
- For musculoskeletal chest pain: rest, anti-inflammatory medications (ibuprofen), and ice/heat
- For heartburn: antacids, avoid lying down after meals, elevate head during sleep
- For anxiety-related chest tightness: deep breathing, reassurance, and stress management
- Avoid caffeine, energy drinks, and stimulants if palpitations are frequent
- Keep a diary of palpitation episodes: timing, duration, associated symptoms, and triggers
- Reduce caffeine and ensure adequate sleep to minimize palpitation frequency
- Do NOT attempt to self-manage chest pain that is new, severe, or unusual — seek evaluation
