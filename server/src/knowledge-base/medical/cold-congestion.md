# Cold & Nasal Congestion

## Symptoms
- Runny nose with clear or slightly cloudy discharge
- Nasal congestion and blocked airways making breathing through the nose difficult
- Sneezing, often frequent in the early stages
- Sore or scratchy throat, especially on the first day
- Mild cough, usually dry initially then productive
- Low-grade fever or no fever (distinguishes common cold from flu)
- Mild headache from sinus pressure
- Fatigue and mild body aches
- Loss of smell and taste associated with nasal congestion
- Symptoms typically peak on days 2-3 and resolve within 7-10 days

## Red Flags
- Thick yellow or green nasal discharge lasting more than 10 days with facial pain or pressure (possible sinusitis — may need antibiotics)
- High fever above 102°F (38.9°C) — more consistent with influenza or bacterial infection
- Symptoms that initially improve then suddenly worsen after day 5 (secondary bacterial infection)
- Severe unilateral facial pain with fever (possible bacterial sinusitis)
- Ear pain, hearing loss, or discharge developing during a cold (secondary ear infection)
- Difficulty breathing beyond nasal congestion (possible lower respiratory involvement)
- Cold symptoms lasting more than 14 days without improvement

## When to Escalate
- If symptoms persist beyond 10-14 days without improvement
- If there is significant facial pain, pressure, or swelling (possible sinusitis)
- If the student develops ear pain or hearing changes
- If there is high fever that the student cannot manage at home
- If cough becomes productive with colored sputum and the student has underlying respiratory conditions

## Self-Care
- Saline nasal rinse or spray to flush out mucus and allergens — very effective for congestion
- Decongestants (pseudoephedrine or oxymetazoline nasal spray — limit nasal spray to 3 days)
- Antihistamines (loratadine or cetirizine) if there is sneezing and watery discharge
- Steam inhalation for 10-15 minutes to ease congestion
- Drink warm fluids: chicken soup, honey-lemon tea, and warm water
- Rest adequately — the immune system works best when rested
- Honey (1 teaspoon) soothes throat and reduces cough (not for children under 1 year)
- Vitamin C and zinc supplements may slightly reduce cold duration if started early
- Wash hands frequently to prevent spreading the virus
- Antibiotics do NOT work for the common cold — it is caused by a virus
