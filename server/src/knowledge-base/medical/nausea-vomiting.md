# Nausea & Vomiting

## Symptoms
- Queasy, unsettled feeling in the stomach preceding the urge to vomit
- Repeated vomiting with or without food content
- Excessive saliva production before vomiting
- Dizziness and lightheadedness accompanying nausea
- Pale skin, cold sweat, and weakness during nausea episodes
- Loss of appetite and aversion to food smells or tastes
- Nausea worsening with movement (motion sickness), strong smells, or after eating
- Abdominal cramping alongside nausea

## Red Flags
- Vomiting blood or material resembling coffee grounds (hematemesis — emergency)
- Projectile vomiting with severe headache and stiff neck (possible meningitis or raised intracranial pressure)
- Vomiting following a head injury — even hours later
- Inability to keep any fluids down for more than 8 hours with signs of dehydration
- Repeated vomiting with severe abdominal pain (possible surgical emergency)
- Vomiting with high fever above 103°F
- Signs of severe dehydration: no urination, rapid heartbeat, extreme weakness
- Vomiting in a student with known diabetes (possible diabetic ketoacidosis)

## When to Escalate
- If there is blood in vomit
- If vomiting is associated with a severe headache or neurological symptoms
- If the student has not kept any fluids down for more than 6-8 hours
- If there are signs of dehydration and oral rehydration is failing
- If vomiting occurs after a head injury

## Self-Care
- Rest and avoid food until the acute vomiting phase passes (usually 1-2 hours)
- Sip clear fluids slowly: water, ORS, clear broth, ginger tea, or diluted apple juice
- Start with small sips (1-2 tablespoons every 5-10 minutes) rather than large amounts
- Eat small amounts of bland foods once vomiting settles: toast, crackers, bananas, boiled rice
- Ginger (ginger tea, ginger candy, or ginger capsules) is evidence-based for nausea relief
- Over-the-counter antiemetics (dimenhydrinate / promethazine) can reduce vomiting episodes
- Avoid strong food smells, spicy food, alcohol, and caffeine
- Rest in a semi-upright position rather than lying flat
- Fresh air and cool compresses to the forehead can ease nausea
- Avoid screen time and reading during nausea episodes (worsens motion-related nausea)
