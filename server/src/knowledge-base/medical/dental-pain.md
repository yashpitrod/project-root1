# Dental Pain & Oral Issues

## Symptoms
- Toothache: sharp, throbbing, or constant pain in or around a tooth
- Pain worsening with hot, cold, or sweet foods and drinks
- Swelling of the gum around the affected tooth
- Pain when biting or chewing on the affected side
- Bad breath or foul taste in the mouth (possible infection or abscess)
- Sensitivity that lingers after the stimulus is removed
- Visible cavity, chip, or crack in a tooth
- Bleeding gums during brushing or flossing

## Red Flags
- Facial swelling extending from the jaw to the eye or neck (spreading dental abscess — emergency)
- Fever with dental pain (systemic infection from dental abscess)
- Difficulty opening mouth or swallowing due to swelling (Ludwig's angina risk)
- Uncontrolled bleeding after tooth extraction or dental trauma
- Knocked-out permanent tooth (dental avulsion — time-critical, bring tooth in milk)
- Jaw locked open or closed after yawning or trauma (TMJ dislocation)

## When to Escalate
- If there is facial swelling with fever — dental abscess may need antibiotics and drainage
- If a tooth has been knocked out — place in milk or saline and seek dental care within 30 minutes for best outcome
- If there is difficulty breathing or swallowing due to oral swelling
- If pain is severe and not controlled by over-the-counter painkillers
- If bleeding does not stop after 30 minutes of firm pressure with gauze

## Self-Care
- Take ibuprofen (400mg with food) for pain and inflammation; paracetamol can be alternated
- Rinse mouth with warm salt water (half teaspoon salt in 1 cup warm water) every 2-3 hours
- Apply clove oil (eugenol) to the affected area with a cotton ball for temporary pain relief
- Apply a cold compress to the outside of the cheek for 15 minutes to reduce swelling
- Avoid very hot, cold, or sweet foods and drinks
- Maintain gentle brushing and flossing around (not directly on) the painful area
- Do not place aspirin directly on the gum — it causes chemical burns
- Schedule a dental appointment as soon as possible — campus dispensary can provide referral
