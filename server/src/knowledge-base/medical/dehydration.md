# Dehydration

## Symptoms
- Increased thirst and dry mouth
- Dark yellow or amber-colored urine
- Reduced urine output (urinating less than usual)
- Fatigue, drowsiness, and low energy
- Dizziness or lightheadedness, especially when standing up quickly
- Dry skin that does not bounce back quickly when pinched (poor skin turgor)
- Headache, often dull and persistent
- Muscle cramps, especially in the legs
- Rapid heartbeat

## Red Flags
- No urination for 8 or more hours
- Very dark urine or no urine output at all
- Rapid, weak pulse and rapid breathing
- Severe dizziness, confusion, or fainting
- Sunken eyes and very dry mucous membranes
- Skin that remains tented when pinched (severe dehydration)
- Seizures in the setting of dehydration
- Dehydration in a student with diabetes, kidney disease, or taking diuretics

## When to Escalate
- If the student is unable to keep any oral fluids down due to vomiting
- If there has been no urination for 8+ hours despite drinking fluids
- If the student is confused, lethargic, or has fainted
- If dehydration is occurring alongside high fever, severe diarrhea, or heavy physical exertion (heatstroke risk)
- If the student has a chronic condition that dehydration could worsen

## Self-Care
- Drink water consistently throughout the day — do not wait until thirsty
- For mild dehydration: drink ORS (oral rehydration solution) or make a homemade version (6 teaspoons sugar + half teaspoon salt in 1 liter water)
- Eat water-rich fruits: watermelon, oranges, cucumbers, grapes
- Avoid caffeinated beverages and alcohol — they increase fluid loss
- During hot weather or physical activity, drink extra fluids before, during, and after
- Monitor urine color: aim for pale straw-yellow
- Set reminders to drink water if you tend to forget during long study sessions
- Carry a reusable water bottle to classes and the library
- In cases of exercise or heat-related dehydration: rest in a cool, shaded area and sip fluids slowly
- Coconut water and diluted fruit juices are good alternatives to plain water for replacing electrolytes
