# Sore Throat

## Symptoms
- Pain or scratchy sensation in the throat that worsens with swallowing
- Swollen, red tonsils sometimes with white patches or streaks of pus
- Hoarse or muffled voice
- Swollen and tender lymph nodes (glands) in the neck
- Mild fever accompanying throat pain
- Difficulty swallowing food or liquids
- Dry throat especially in the morning after sleeping

## Red Flags
- Severe difficulty swallowing or inability to swallow saliva (drooling)
- Difficulty breathing or a feeling of throat closing
- High fever above 103°F (39.4°C) with throat pain
- Inability to open mouth fully (trismus), which may indicate peritonsillar abscess
- Swelling visible on one side of the throat or neck
- Throat pain lasting more than 7 days without improvement
- Blood in saliva or phlegm
- Recurrent strep throat infections (3 or more episodes in 6 months)

## When to Escalate
- If rapid strep test is positive or bacterial infection is suspected
- If the student cannot eat or drink for more than 24 hours due to throat pain
- If there is a visible abscess or asymmetric swelling in the throat
- If the student has a history of rheumatic fever (strep must be treated promptly)
- If symptoms do not improve after 3-4 days of self-care

## Self-Care
- Gargle with warm salt water (half teaspoon salt in 1 cup warm water) every 2-3 hours
- Drink warm fluids such as herbal tea with honey, warm water with lemon
- Use throat lozenges or hard candy to keep the throat moist
- Take paracetamol or ibuprofen for pain relief
- Use a humidifier in the room or take a hot shower for steam
- Avoid irritants such as smoking, dust, and very spicy or acidic foods
- Rest the voice and avoid shouting or prolonged talking
- Stay hydrated with small, frequent sips of water throughout the day
