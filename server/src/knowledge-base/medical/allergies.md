# Allergies & Allergic Reactions

## Symptoms
- Sneezing, runny nose, and nasal congestion (allergic rhinitis)
- Itchy, watery, red eyes (allergic conjunctivitis)
- Itchy skin, hives (raised red welts), or localized rash
- Mild swelling around eyes, lips, or face
- Coughing and wheezing triggered by allergen exposure
- Itchy throat or ears
- Skin reactions after contact with certain plants, chemicals, or cosmetics (contact dermatitis)
- Symptoms worsen during pollen season or in dusty environments

## Red Flags
- Swelling of the tongue, throat, or airway causing difficulty breathing or swallowing (anaphylaxis)
- Rapid drop in blood pressure: dizziness, lightheadedness, fainting
- Widespread hives covering large body areas that spread rapidly
- Tightness in chest, wheezing, or inability to speak in full sentences
- Nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain following known allergen exposure (food, insect sting, medication)
- Known history of anaphylaxis with repeat exposure to the same allergen
- Blue discoloration of lips or fingertips (cyanosis)

## When to Escalate
- Any sign of anaphylaxis — this is a medical emergency, administer epinephrine if available and call emergency services immediately
- If the student has a known severe allergy and has been exposed to the allergen
- If respiratory symptoms (wheezing, difficulty breathing) develop alongside skin symptoms
- If hives or swelling do not respond to antihistamines within 1-2 hours
- If the student is experiencing their first-ever allergic reaction and is anxious or symptoms are progressing

## Self-Care
- Identify and avoid known allergens (keep a diary of triggers)
- Take oral antihistamines (cetirizine 10mg once daily, or loratadine 10mg once daily) for mild symptoms
- For itchy eyes: use cold compresses and avoid rubbing; over-the-counter antihistamine eye drops may help
- For nasal symptoms: saline nasal spray or rinse to clear allergens
- For skin reactions: apply calamine lotion or hydrocortisone cream (1%) to affected areas
- Keep windows closed during high pollen days; shower after outdoor activities
- Wash bedding weekly in hot water to reduce dust mite exposure
- Students with known severe allergies should carry an epinephrine auto-injector at all times and ensure roommates know how to use it
