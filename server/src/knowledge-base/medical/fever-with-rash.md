# Fever with Rash

## Symptoms
- Fever occurring together with a skin rash
- Rash may be widespread or localized, flat or raised
- Red or pink spots that may coalesce into larger patches
- Itching associated with the rash
- Generalized body aches and headache with fever
- Swollen lymph nodes in the neck, armpits, or groin
- Rash appearing 1-5 days after fever onset (viral exanthem pattern)

## Red Flags
- Non-blanching (petechial or purpuric) rash that does not turn white when pressed — possible meningococcal disease or other serious systemic infection — EMERGENCY
- Rash with high fever, stiff neck, and extreme headache — possible meningitis — EMERGENCY
- Widespread blistering rash (vesicles or bullae) affecting the mouth, eyes, or genitals — possible Stevens-Johnson Syndrome — EMERGENCY
- Rash starting on the face and spreading rapidly to the trunk and limbs with high fever
- Rash in a returning traveler from malaria-endemic areas or tropical regions
- Rash with severe joint pain and swelling (possible dengue, chikungunya, or autoimmune cause)
- Honey-colored crusted rash spreading rapidly on the face (impetigo — needs antibiotics)

## When to Escalate
- Any non-blanching rash with fever — treat as emergency, do not wait
- Any rash with mucosal involvement (inside the mouth, eyes, or genitals)
- Rash with high fever and neurological symptoms
- Chickenpox (vesicular rash starting on trunk) in an immunocompromised student — needs antiviral treatment urgently
- Rash that has spread significantly within 24 hours despite no fever improvement

## Self-Care
- For mild viral rash with low-grade fever: rest, antihistamines for itching, and monitor
- Keep the skin cool — avoid hot baths and heavy clothing
- Apply calamine lotion for itch relief
- Take paracetamol for fever management
- Avoid scratching the rash to prevent secondary infection
- Keep the affected area clean and dry
- Isolate from other students if the rash might be contagious (chickenpox, etc.)
- Do NOT ignore fever-rash combinations — seek evaluation to rule out serious causes
