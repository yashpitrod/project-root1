# Diarrhea & Gastroenteritis

## Symptoms
- Frequent loose or watery stools (3 or more times in 24 hours)
- Abdominal cramps and urgency before bowel movements
- Nausea and sometimes vomiting
- Bloating and excessive gas
- Low-grade fever (99-101°F)
- Loss of appetite
- Dehydration signs: increased thirst, dry mouth, reduced urination
- Fatigue and weakness from fluid loss

## Red Flags
- Blood or mucus in stool (possible dysentery or inflammatory bowel disease)
- High fever above 102°F (39°C) with diarrhea
- Severe dehydration: no urination for 8+ hours, dry mouth, rapid heartbeat, dizziness on standing, sunken eyes
- Diarrhea lasting more than 3 days without improvement
- Severe abdominal pain that is constant rather than crampy
- Recent antibiotic use with new-onset diarrhea (possible C. difficile infection)
- Inability to keep any oral fluids down for more than 6 hours
- Recent travel to areas with poor sanitation

## When to Escalate
- If the student shows signs of moderate-to-severe dehydration
- If there is blood or pus in the stool
- If diarrhea persists beyond 48 hours with no sign of improvement
- If the student has a chronic condition (diabetes, kidney disease) that dehydration could worsen
- If there is associated high fever
- If multiple students in the same hostel are affected (potential outbreak)

## Self-Care
- Oral rehydration is the top priority: sip ORS solution frequently, or make a homemade version (6 teaspoons sugar + half teaspoon salt in 1 liter clean water)
- Follow the BRAT diet: bananas, rice, applesauce, toast — bland and easy to digest
- Avoid dairy products, caffeine, alcohol, spicy food, and high-fiber foods until recovery
- Probiotics (yogurt with live cultures, or probiotic capsules) may help restore gut flora
- Zinc supplements (20mg daily for 10-14 days) can reduce duration and severity
- Anti-diarrheal medication (loperamide 2mg) only if there is no fever and no blood in stool
- Wash hands thoroughly after using the bathroom and before eating
- Avoid preparing food for others while symptomatic
- Monitor stool frequency and hydration status (urine color should be pale yellow)
