# Menstrual Cramps & Reproductive Health

## Symptoms
- Cramping pain in the lower abdomen, usually starting just before or at the beginning of menstruation
- Dull, continuous ache in the lower back and thighs
- Nausea and sometimes vomiting during severe cramps
- Headache and dizziness during menstruation
- Bloating and water retention
- Fatigue and mood changes (irritability, sadness)
- Loose stools or diarrhea during the first 1-2 days of the period
- Breast tenderness before and during menstruation

## Red Flags
- Extremely heavy bleeding: soaking through a pad or tampon every hour for more than 2 consecutive hours
- Severe pain that does not respond to over-the-counter painkillers and prevents attending classes
- Fever during menstruation (possible infection)
- Sudden change in menstrual pattern: much heavier, longer, or more painful than usual
- Missed periods for 3 or more consecutive months without pregnancy (amenorrhea — may indicate underlying condition)
- Foul-smelling discharge with pain (possible infection)
- Fainting or severe dizziness during menstruation (possible anemia from heavy bleeding)
- Severe pelvic pain outside of menstruation (possible ovarian cyst, endometriosis, or ectopic pregnancy)

## When to Escalate
- If pain is severe enough to cause vomiting or prevent daily activities despite medication
- If there is heavy bleeding with signs of anemia (pale skin, extreme fatigue, shortness of breath)
- If periods have stopped for 3+ months and the student is not pregnant
- If there is suspicion of pregnancy with pelvic pain (possible ectopic pregnancy — emergency)
- If menstrual cramps are getting progressively worse over months (possible endometriosis)

## Self-Care
- Take ibuprofen (400mg with food) starting 1-2 days before expected period onset for best cramp prevention
- Apply a heating pad or hot water bottle to the lower abdomen for 15-20 minutes
- Gentle exercise: walking, stretching, or yoga can reduce cramp severity
- Stay hydrated and eat light, nutritious meals
- Avoid excessive caffeine and salt, which can worsen bloating
- Practice relaxation techniques and deep breathing
- Track menstrual cycle using an app to anticipate and prepare for symptoms
- Over-the-counter antispasmodics (mefenamic acid) can help if ibuprofen alone is insufficient
- Iron-rich foods (spinach, lentils, jaggery) to prevent anemia from heavy periods
