# Fatigue & Exhaustion

## Symptoms
- Persistent tiredness that does not improve with rest
- Difficulty getting out of bed in the morning despite adequate sleep hours
- Low energy throughout the day, especially in the afternoon
- Reduced ability to concentrate or stay focused during lectures and study
- Physical weakness and heaviness in the limbs
- Feeling drained after mild exertion (climbing stairs, short walks)
- Cognitive fog: slow thinking, poor memory, difficulty making decisions
- Lack of motivation for academic work, social activities, or hobbies
- Disrupted sleep: difficulty sleeping despite tiredness, or sleeping excessively

## Red Flags
- Fatigue with unexplained weight loss (more than 5kg in 1-2 months)
- Fatigue with night sweats and fever (possible infection or malignancy)
- Fatigue with significant shortness of breath on minimal exertion
- Extreme pallor — very pale skin, pale inner eyelids (possible severe anemia)
- Fatigue after a recent viral illness lasting more than 4 weeks (possible post-viral syndrome)
- Fatigue with jaundice (yellow skin/eyes) — possible liver issue
- Fatigue with frequent thirst and urination (possible undiagnosed diabetes)
- Fatigue with severe depression or suicidal thoughts

## When to Escalate
- If fatigue is persistent for more than 3-4 weeks without an obvious cause
- If there is unexplained weight loss with fatigue
- If pallor is significant (possible anemia — refer for blood tests)
- If fatigue is worsening rather than improving over time
- If the student suspects a chronic condition such as thyroid disorder or diabetes

## Self-Care
- Ensure 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night — poor sleep is the most common cause of fatigue in students
- Maintain a consistent sleep schedule including weekends
- Eat regular, nutritious meals: iron-rich foods (spinach, lentils, meat) and B12 sources (eggs, dairy)
- Stay well hydrated — even mild dehydration reduces energy significantly
- Regular physical activity paradoxically increases energy; even a 20-minute walk helps
- Limit caffeine and energy drinks — they cause energy crashes and disrupt sleep
- Take breaks during study sessions: 5-10 minutes every hour
- Address stress and anxiety, which are major contributors to fatigue
- Reduce alcohol consumption — alcohol disrupts sleep quality significantly
- Check iron, vitamin D, and thyroid levels if fatigue is persistent (blood test through dispensary)
